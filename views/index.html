Travel ideas

A visit to the South of France should be part of a larger European extravaganza. We highly recommend allowing yourself time to recover from jetlag by visiting your port of entry for a few days before venturing into Provence. Here are a few ideas to start your mind wandering.

  • France

    Paris is always a good idea. -Audrey Hepburn

    Not only is Paris is the most logical port of entry for the purposes of our wedding, but there are few places more beautiful. Paris teems with history, life and culture in a way that forces you to slow down and enjoy life—something completely lost in American culture. There are up to four flights daily between SFO and CDG, and high-speed TGV trains can whisk you to Avignon in about two hours, making Paris an easy place to start.

    To the north: Normandy, home to several WWII sites as well as captivating Mont-Saint-Michel. To the west you'll find the Loire Valley: château country, and further west is the rugged coastline of Brittany. To the east, the rolling hills of Champagne, followed by the German-influenced border towns of Strasbourg and Colmar. To the south you'll find the sun-drenched regions of Provence and the Riviera—the Côte d'Azur. Catch all the posh seaside culture you can stand in Saint-Tropez, Antibes, Nice, and Monaco.

    Provence is dotted with small and mid-sized cities, many of which are heavily Roman influenced. The terrain is often rugged, offering dramatic landscapes overlooking vineyards, orchards and a variety of crops. Popular mid-sized cities in Provence include Avignon, Aix-en-Provence, Arles, and Nimes. Some of the smaller villages—the kind that really envoke the essence of Provence—include St-Rémy de Provence, L'Isle-sur-la-Sorgue, Fontaine-de-Vaucluse, Les Baux de Provence, and Bédoin. The Lubéron lies on the eastern border of Provence, closer to the Alps and is home to villages like Bonnieux, Gordes, Lacoste, Ménerbes, and the famous Roussillon.

  • England

    …when a man is tired of London, he is tired of life; for there is in London all that life can afford. -Samuel Johnson

    London is a city of incredible diversity and a fascinating mix of historic and modern. It's a massive, ever-changing city with something for everyone. There are six flights daily from SFO to Heathrow, and Eurostar operates direct high-speed rail service between St Pancras and Avignon, making London a great place to start.

  • The Netherlands

    Amsterdam, like San Francisco, is a compact city with a lot of character and a laissez-faire attitude. It's packed with quirky architecture, orderly canals, and very walkable. Most Dutch people speak English better than most Americans, making it an easy place to visit. KLM operates daily flights from SFO to Amsterdam, and Avignon is accessible via high-speed rail with a change in Paris.

  • Italy

    Northern Italy is an option for those visiting the Côte d'Azur after the wedding (specifically Nice). Italian Riveria towns just beyond the border include San Remo, Bordighera, and Genoa. Milan and Pisa are further afield, but still accessible by train. Anything beyond Milan would be better by air.

  • Ireland

    Dublin is a small, vibrant city that represents contemporary Ireland. Meanwhile, the countryside is easily accessible by car, allowing visitors to pass through traditional towns and rolling green hills as they cross the Emerald Isle. Aer Lingus operates daily flights between SFO and Dublin. You can then fly from Dublin to Marseille to reach the wedding venue.

How to get there

Getting to La Coquillade does take some effort. You will ultimately arrive to the gates of La Coquillade by car, but you have options leading up to this final stage of the journey.

  1. Step 1: getting to Europe

    Amsterdam, London, Paris, and Zurich are the easiest ports of entry with a high-speed rail connection to the South of France.

    There are also non-stop flights between San Francisco and Copenhagen, Dublin, Frankfurt, Istanbul, and Munich. Each of these cities also has non-stop flights to the South of France.

  2. Step 2: make your way to the South of France

    By rail

    Avignon TGV is the rail gateway to Provence and has on-site car rental facilities. Drive time to La Coquillade is approximately one-and-a-half hours.

    By air

    Marseille (MRS) is the largest airport serving Provence and has on-site car rental facilities. Drive time to La Coquillade is approximately two hours. Note that Marseille is the second-largest city in France and therefore subject to traffic.

  3. Step 3: getting to La Coquillade

    We highly recommend you map the route on your phone using Google Maps (this would of course require an international data plan). La Coquillade is not listed in most rental car GPS systems and does not have an address. Keep your eyes peeled for a sign pointing you in the right direction as you approach the area.
